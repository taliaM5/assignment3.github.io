The Spider-Verse


Welcome to the Spider-Verse, where heroes swing across dimensions, weaving a web of adventure and inspiration.

Earth-1610, home to Miles Morales

Earth-1610 is an alternate reality featuring Miles Morales, also known as Spider-Man, and his predecessor, a perfect version of Peter Parker. This universe is the primary setting of "Spider-Man: Into The Spider-Verse" and the short film "The Spider Within," as well as appearing in the sequel, "Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse."

The universe is notable for being home to two Spider-People and Wilson Fisk, who opened a gateway between universes using the super-collider. Peter Parker in this universe lived a perfect life as the beloved protector of New York until his death at 26 in 2018. Following his demise, Miles Morales inherited the mantle of Spider-Man.

Miles is forced into heroism to stop Kingpin and prevent a black hole from swallowing Brooklyn. He teams up with alternate versions of Spider-Man to overcome challenges. Notable locations include Brooklyn Visions Academy, Fisk Tower, and Lenny's Deli & Grocery.

Notable differences in this universe include Peter Parker's blond hair, his merchandise popularity, and the absence of negative press from The Daily Bugle. Miles Morales becomes Spider-Man after Peter's death, and there are various cultural differences such as the renaming of brands like Coca Cola to Koka Soda and FedEx to RedEx.

Earth-65, home to Spider-Gwen

Earth-65 is the dimension where Gwen Stacy, also known as Spider-Woman, resides. In this reality's Chelsea, New York, Gwen, a member of The Mary Janes, was bitten by a radioactive spider, granting her superpowers. She becomes Spider-Woman and faces pursuit by law enforcement after the death of her best friend, Peter Parker.

Key Locations: New York City, Manhattan, Chelsea, Visions Academy, Stacy Residence,Guggenheim Museum

Notable Differences: The sky appears pink instead of blue. Environmental colors shift with the emotions of the inhabitants, especially Gwen Stacy. The world resembles a watercolor painting. Peter attended Visions Academy instead of Midtown High School. Gwen Stacy, not Peter Parker, was bitten by the radioactive spider and became Spider-Woman. Without Peter becoming Spider-Man, he transformed into The Lizard, indirectly killed by Spider-Woman. Consequently, Ben Parker remains alive. The NYPD is referred to as PDNY (Police Department of New York).

Earth-50101, home to the city of Mumbattan and Spider-Man India

Earth-50101 is Pavitr Prabhakar's home dimension, where he operates as the superhero Spider-Man, wielding spider-like powers to protect the city of Mumbattan. The universe features landmarks like Mumbattan, a fusion of Mumbai and Manhattan, and institutions such as Alchemax and Mumbattan Visions Academy. Pavitr resides with his aunt Maya, and the dimension includes unique locales like Bronxnagar.

Notably, many inhabitants are Indian counterparts of familiar characters, such as Gayatri Singh (Gwen Stacy) and Maya Prabhakar (May Parker).

Earth-928, home to the Spider Society HQ in Nueva York and Spider-Man 2099

Earth-928 serves as the native dimension of Miguel O'Hara, alias the superhero Spider-Man, and acts as the headquarters for the Spider-Society. The world boasts advanced features such as hover cars and a lunar-bound train.

Key Distinctions: New York is referred to as Nueva York. The setting is in the year 2099. Spider-Man's powers stem from a drug rather than a spider bite.

Earth-42, the universe where the spider that bit Miles originated

In the universe of Earth-42, there are no Spider-People, and crime runs rampant in New York City. When a radioactive spider intended for Miles Morales is inadvertently pulled into another universe by Jonathan Ohnn using the Super-Collider, it disrupts the natural order. Without a Spider-Man to protect the city, crime spreads uncontrollably, plunging New York into chaos.

Following the death of Captain Jeff Morales, his brother Aaron Davis assumes responsibility for his nephew Miles. Aaron mentors Miles in the ways of crime, leading him to become the Prowler instead of Aaron himself. The absence of Spider-Man alters the dynamics of this universe significantly, with crime syndicates like the Sinister Six/Sinister Six Cartel dominating the landscape.

Notable differences in Earth-42 include Miles Morales assuming the mantle of the Prowler, the absence of Spider-Man, a distinct color scheme dominated by red, green, and purple, widespread crime throughout New York, and alterations in brand names such as M&Ms being called W&Ws and Coca Cola referred to as Soda Kola. Additionally, while Comic Con exists, it is known as Comics Con in this universe.

This universe is a unique blend of 1970s and 1980s London vibes with a modern New York twist. Hobie comes from Camden, New London, a vibrant neighborhood that feels like a living collage of punk-rock posters.
In the realm of Earth-22191 resides Margo Kess, known as Spider-Byte. In this dimension, citizens predominantly inhabit virtual avatars within the Cyberspace, shaping their existence.
Earth-13122 is portrayed in stop-motion animation, reflecting the universe featured in LEGO video games centered around Marvel characters.
Earth-616, also known as Earth-616B, is a significant universe where Spider-Man, his wife Mary Jane Watson and daughter Mayday, and numerous adversaries reside, amidst a world where superhumans are integrated into everyday life in New York City.
