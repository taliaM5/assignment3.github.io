The Characters

Welcome to our guide to the iconic characters of "Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse"! Meet Miles Morales, a relatable Brooklyn teen who becomes Spider-Man after a radioactive spider bite. Join him as he teams up with alternate versions of Spider-Man from different dimensions, including Peter B. Parker and Gwen Stacy, to save all of reality from a cosmic threat. Explore their diverse backgrounds, powers, and personalities in this thrilling animated adventure that celebrates the hero within us all.

Miles Morales

Miles Morales, a student at Brooklyn Visions Academy, gained spider-like powers after being bitten by a radioactive spider. Reluctant at first, he became the masked vigilante Spider-Man following the death of Peter Parker. With the help of the Spider-Gang, Miles saved the city from the Super-Collider and became Brooklyn's new protector.

As he continued his heroics, Miles faced dangerous supervillains and gained a new suit, becoming an icon in New York like his predecessor. However, he also faced scrutiny from The Daily Bugle, led by J. Jonah Jameson, who portrayed him as a criminal mastermind.

A year and a half later, Miles encountered Jonathan Ohnn, now known as Spot, who threatened to destroy everything Miles loves by traveling between alternate dimensions. Alongside Gwen Stacy, Miles joined the Spider-Society to stop Spot. When Miles learned his father was in danger, he defied the Society's rules and used a machine to return home, but ended up in another dimension instead.

Gwen Stacy

Gwen Stacy, a student at Visions Academy, gained superhuman abilities after being bitten by a radioactive spider, becoming the superheroine Spider-Woman. She defended her city against villains like Doctor Octopus and the Vulture but struggled with guilt after failing to save her friend Peter Parker. Pulled into another dimension by Kingpin's Super-Collider, she met alternate versions of Peter and befriended Miles Morales. Together with other Spider-Heroes, they stopped Kingpin, but Gwen returned home feeling isolated and missing her new friends.

Recruited by Miguel O'Hara and Jessica Drew into the Spider-Society, Gwen protected the Multiverse, befriending Hobie Brown and Pavitr Prabhakar. Sent to track down a villain, she couldn't resist reuniting with Miles, inadvertently empowering the villain further. When Miles discovered his father's impending death, Gwen reluctantly joined the chase to capture him. After Miles escaped, Gwen repaired her relationship with her father and obtained a Multiversal Gizmo. Promising to rescue Miles, she assembled a new Spider-Gang and embarked on a mission across dimensions.

Spider-Punk

Hobart "Hobie" Brown gained spider-like powers after a radioactive spider bite and became the vigilante Spider-Man to resist the oppressive regime of Earth-138. His rebellious nature earned him the nickname Spider-Punk as he fought against the corrupt government, making both enemies and allies. He joined the Spider-Society but later quit after aiding Miles in escaping, triggering chaos in Nueva York.

Pavitr Prabhakar

Pavitr Prabhakar, a student at Mumbattan Visions Academy, gained spider-like abilities and became the superhero Spider-Man, fighting crime in Mumbathan. Balancing heroics with high school life, he joined the Spider-Society at Miguel O'Hara's invitation. Pavitr later aided Gwen Stacy, Hobie Brown, and an alternate Spider-Man against the Spot, a villain with multiversal powers. Despite their efforts, they couldn't stop Spot's plan. Pavitr saved his girlfriend but couldn't rescue her father in time. He then joined the reformed Spider-Gang to find Miles Morales and assist against Miguel and Spot, as a token of gratitude for Miles' help.

Peter B. Parker

Peter Benjamin "B." Parker's life took a dramatic turn when he was bitten by a radioactive spider, gaining superhuman abilities and becoming the masked superhero Spider-Man. Balancing heroics with personal relationships, he faced numerous villains while working as a photographer at The Daily Bugle. Despite personal tragedies, including the death of his uncle and girlfriend Gwen Stacy, he eventually married Mary Jane Watson. However, his life became more complicated when he bonded with a symbiote, becoming more violent. After defeating Venom and facing conflicts with his friend Harry Osborn as the Green Goblin, Peter's life took a toll, especially after the death of his Aunt May. Guilt-ridden, he isolated himself from his loved ones as Spider-Man.

Displaced to another universe, Peter mentored teenager Miles Morales to become Spider-Man. Alongside an alternate Gwen Stacy, they formed a team to return to their respective realities, facing challenges and ultimately sacrificing himself to save the multiverse. Miles emerged as the new Spider-Man, inspiring Peter to mend his relationships and become a better person, even becoming a father to Mayday Parker. Joining the Spider-Society, Peter balanced his responsibilities as a superhero and a father while facing threats to the multiverse. However, after failing to prevent the destruction of a universe, Peter and the Spider-Society vowed to prevent further catastrophes. Reuniting with Gwen and Miles, Peter joined a new Spider-Gang to rescue Miles, embracing his role as a mentor and friend once again.

Miguel O'Hara

Miguel O'Hara, a genetic scientist at Alchemax, gained superhuman abilities after a sabotage incident on a project he worked on. He became Spider-Man of the year 2099, protecting Nueva York alongside his AI companion, LYLA. Upon discovering the multiverse, Miguel established the Spider-Society to prevent its collapse and return stranded villains to their universes.

Despite his efforts, Miguel suffered trauma when his universe was destroyed due to his actions. He replaced his alternate universe counterpart to raise his daughter but became a more serious and work-focused person. Learning about the Canon Events, Miguel enforced strict rules within the Spider-Society, prohibiting interaction with anomalies like Miles Morales from Earth-1610.

Conflict arose when the Spider-Society clashed with the Spot and Miles Morales, who attempted to save his father. Despite trying to reason with Miles, he escaped back to his universe, prompting Miguel to pursue him to prevent further damage.

Jessica Drew

Jessica Drew is a key ally of Spider-Man 2099 in the Spider-Society, prioritizing the stability of the multiverse above all else. She empathizes with the struggles of Miles Morales and Gwen Stacy, despite her dedication to preserving canon events. In "Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse," Jessica quickly bonds with Gwen and joins Miguel's team to protect the multiverse.

Although reluctant to chase down Miles, she agrees to help apprehend him. The cliffhanger ending suggests a potential shift in allegiances for Jessica in "Spider-Man: Beyond the Spider-Verse," possibly inspired by Peter B. Parker's choice to side with Miles. Her fatherhood experience and natural sympathy for Miles and Gwen could lead her to become the new guardian of the Spider-Verse once conflicts are resolved.

The Spot

Former scientist Johnathon at Alchemax inadvertently transformed Miles Morales into Spider-Man by transporting a radioactive spider across dimensions. Exposed to dark matter during the collider's destruction, he mutated into a dimension-traveling supervillain, posing a significant threat to the Multiverse and becoming Miles' arch-nemesis.

Mayday Parker/Spider-Girl

May "Mayday" Parker, daughter of Peter Benjamin Parker and Mary Jane Watson-Parker, inherited her father's powers and often accompanied him on his Spider-Man duties, showcasing a sociable nature and lightening tense situations with her giggles.

Ben Reilly/Scarlet Spider

The character is enlisted into Miguel O'Hara's Spider Society alongside other multiversal Spider-Men, and he has a tendency to narrate his actions with exaggerated exposition.

Spider-Byte

Margo becomes a member of Miguel O'Hara's Spider Society, preferring to spend her time as an avatar projection at its Earth-928 headquarters. Spider-Byte joins Spider-Woman's team to rescue Miles after being mistakenly sent to Earth-42 by the Machine instead of his home reality.

Miles Morales (EARTH 42)

Miles Gonzalo Morales, a teenager from Brooklyn and nephew of Aaron Davis, narrowly missed being bitten by a radioactive spider, which was pulled to another universe. With New York overrun by supervillains due to the absence of Spider-Man, Miles, after his father's death, adopts the persona of the anti-hero Prowler.

Ganke Lee

Ganke Lee attends Brooklyn Visions Academy and shares a room with Miles.

Rio Morales (Earth 1610)

Rio Morales, wife of Jefferson Davis and mother of Miles Morales, serves as a pillar of strength and encouragement in Miles' life, providing vital support during challenging times and helping him stay grounded when he feels overwhelmed.

Jefferson Davis

Officer Jefferson "Jeff" Davis serves in the New York Police Department in Brooklyn and is Miles Morales' father.

Aaron Davis (Earth-42)

Aaron Davis is the uncle of Miles Morales and younger brother of the deceased Jefferson Davis. He is someone who lives a life of crime who became the mentor of his nephew when he became Prowler.
